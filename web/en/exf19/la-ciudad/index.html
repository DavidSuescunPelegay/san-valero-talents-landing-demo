GO MADRIDING!

IF YOU MAKE IT TO #EXF19 FROM OUTSIDE MADRID, YOU HAVE TO MAKE TIME TO DISCOVER THE CITY

MADRID IN JUNE

In June you can already feel the sun rays on your skin, the days getting longer and the nice weather making bard terraces full to the brim. The atmosphere smells of the end of spring, where a walk in Retiro Park is the perfect plan to put and end to your work day. During the first six months of the year, Madrid fills up with color for you to hit the streets and enjoy the endless options that the city has on offer. These are some of the plans we recommend:

Teatro Circo Price

The Teatro Circo Price de Madrid is the mecca for circus shows, it also hosts theater and concerts. It takes its name from the one that existed, for almost a century, in the Paseo de Recoletos. At the beginning of the 2000, the City council recovered the old industrial building of a factory, of “neomudéjar style”, to locate the new Teatro Circo Price.

Going out for drinks in Ponzano

Ponzano Street

BEERS AT CONDE DUQUE

Conde Duque Square

The tapas route through Conde Duque is a must in your Madrid to-do list. There are some that call it the “Western Malasaña”. It is an area with a chill atmosphere, from the parks to the squares. It’s great if you’re looking to enjoy a couple of beers around Madrid.

A STROLL THROUGH MALASAÑA

From Bilbao to the Spanish Square

One of neighbourhoods that you can’t miss out on is Malasaña. Wandering. That is the true spirit of this neighbourhood from Madrid. Stroll through the narrow streets, breathe in and relax at its squares, and discover every corner of this district where art, leisure and history collide.

Toledo Bridge

How does a bike path through Madrid sound? A narrow trail that follows the Manzanares River for as long as 7 kilometers through the heart of the city. Sounds terrific, we know.

A TRAVELING EXHIBITION AT THE PRADO MUSEUM

Paseo del Prado

You can’t miss out on the different exhibitions at one of the best museums of the capital: The Prado Museum. So do not overthink it and head over to one of the most important museums in the world as well as one of the most visited ones.

SUNSET AT CÍRCULO DE BELLAS ARTES

42 Alcalá Street

From the rooftop of The Circulo de Bellas Artes we can find one of the most sought out and incredible views of the city. Located in between Alcala street and Gran Via, the rooftop terrace displays the “old town”, el Paseo de la Castellana, el Paseo del Padro, the Cibeles Square, the emblematic Metropolis building and the city chaos.

City of Museums

Have you ever heard of the painting “El Guernica” by Pablo Picasso? This master piece worth the visit alone. Indeed, this museum is full of the requisite Salvador Dalí, Joan Miró, but also it includes a rare and fascinating retrospective of Civil War Propaganda. You have the opportunity to go and spend some time there, it’s just 4minutes far away from Circus Price. 

Reserve recommended

Parque del Retiro

One of the best ways to have a rest, after a hard day is to buy a book at the Salesian Bookstore and spend some time at the wonderful Retiro Park. It was once the domain of the royal circle but is now the city’s prized green lung. The central lake is peppered with rowboats; the towering fence is circled by lithe joggers; and the Cristal Palace, exhibit flora and fauna from the Philippines.

Caixa Forum

The Caixa Forum Museum in Madrid is a cultural center dedicated mainly to temporary exhibitions. It is located in the Paseo del Prado in Madrid and it differs from the rest of the facades since its own is vegetal.

Museo Thyssen-Bornemisza

The Tyssen-Bornemisza Museum is a pinacoteca of ancient and modern masters that was founded in 1992. The Tyssen is part of the Art Triangle, which also includes the Reina Sofía and the Museo del Prado. In this museum you will find a wide collection of works, from Gothic paintings of the fourteenth and fifteenth centuries, to works by Edward Hopper.

Reserve recommended

FLAMENCO

You don’t need to go to Andalucía to experience the raw, bleeding passion of flamenco because the world’s most celebrated tablao is tight here in Madrid. It opened in 1956, and since then they’ve never stop dancing. In addition, during the show you can enjoy a wonderful dinner, with the best Spanish tapas.

Sunset at Templo de Debod

The temple of Debod is a spectacular place to see the best sunset and spend the afternoon. This place was a gift from Egyptian President Gamal Abdel Nasser for the Spanish participation in the Unesco campaign of the 1960s to save the sanctuaries of the Nubian region.

Metrópolis Building

This opulent triangular structure provides a great welcome to all tourists and locals who are visiting Madrid. This building was built between 1907 and 1911 for the company Unión y el Fénix Español, which placed its figure and symbol of Ave Fénix on its dome. In 1972, it was the insurance company Metropolis that acquired the property.

Casa Labra

Have you ever tried the best cod croquetas of the planet? Casa Labra is a centenary tavern, which was founded in 1860 and since then its specialty has always been cod and tapas made with cod. The tavern is located on Calle Tetuán, very close to Sol, so do not waste time and go there to eat some cod croquetas with a glass of Spanish wine.
